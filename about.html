About

Background, education, athletics, and the values that shape my work.

Background

I grew up in Colorado Springs, where I spent my high school years balancing rigorous academics with competitive running. I graduated Summa Cum Laude from Cheyenne Mountain High School in May 2025, ranking 6 out of 286 students in my class.

Today I'm a freshman at Washington University in St. Louis, pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Business and Computer Science through the McKelvey School of Engineering. I run NCAA Division III Cross Country and Track & Field for the Bears, and I'm always looking for ways to combine technology, business, and community impact.

Athletics

Running has shaped how I approach almost everything — discipline, consistency, and showing up when it's hard. At WashU I compete year-round in Cross Country and Indoor/Outdoor Track & Field at the NCAA Division III level.

In high school I earned Nike Indoor All-American honors during the winter of 2025 — a milestone that reflects years of consistent training and racing.

Community

Outside of academics and athletics, I volunteer with the Bear Cubs Running Club in St. Louis — helping local children with special needs build motor and social skills through running. Back home in Colorado, I've volunteered at Springs Rescue Mission, cooking and distributing meals at a local homeless shelter.
